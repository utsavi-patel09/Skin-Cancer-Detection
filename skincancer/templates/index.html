Overview of Skin Cancer Types

Malignant

Malignant skin cancer occurs when cells become cancerous and can spread to other parts of the body. It includes serious types like melanoma.

Benign

Benign skin growths are non-cancerous and do not spread. However, some may require removal if they cause discomfort.

Basic Skin Care Precautions

  • Use sunscreen daily with SPF 30 or higher.
  • Wear protective clothing, hats, and sunglasses when outdoors.
  • Avoid tanning beds and excessive sun exposure.
  • Examine your skin regularly for new or changing growths or spots.
  • Consult a dermatologist annually for skin checks.
